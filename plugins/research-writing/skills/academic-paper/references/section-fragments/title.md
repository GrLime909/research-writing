# Section: Title

## Default Pattern

Prefer concrete titles that combine:

`system / object + action / capability + application or consequence`

Examples of title logic:

- material plus function
- method plus task
- process plus scale
- model plus data regime

## Drafting Rules

- Tell the reader what to expect; do not be a slogan.
- Be searchable: include terms a researcher would search for.
- Be substantiated by data — if a number or quantitative word appears, verify it against the manuscript.
- Create curiosity without sacrificing credibility. A hook is only acceptable if the claim remains fully defensible.

## Things to Avoid

- Grant-style aims (`Toward ...`, `A study of ...`).
- Overbroad field claims (`A new era of ...`, `Revolutionizing ...`).
- Question titles unless the paper genuinely answers the question.
- Jargon a non-specialist in the target audience would not recognize.
- Vague prestige words such as `novel`, `advanced`, `powerful`, `green`, `efficient` unless they are made concrete by the rest of the title.

## When Asked for Alternatives

Generate 3-5 candidates spanning:

1. **Declarative** — states the finding (`X enables Y through Z`).
2. **Finding-led** — leads with the key result (`Improved Y via X for Z application`).
3. **Question** (rarely) — only if the paper genuinely answers it.

Mark the most defensible one. Verify any quantitative claim in each candidate.

## Diagnostics Before Finalizing

- [ ] Is every term in the title substantiated by the manuscript?
- [ ] Would a researcher searching for this topic find this paper?
- [ ] Does the title scope match the evidence scope?
