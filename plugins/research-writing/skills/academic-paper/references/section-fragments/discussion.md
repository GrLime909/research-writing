# Section: Discussion

## Default Structure

`central advance -> what the evidence means -> relation to prior work -> constraints / limitations -> future use or open questions`

## Core Principle

Discussion **interprets**, it does not repeat Results figure by figure. Select the evidence that changes interpretation.

## Drafting Rules

- Address rival explanations before generalizing. Reviewers look for this.
- Hedging strength must match evidence strength. Do not promote a "consistent with" finding to "demonstrates" wording.
- Limitations come from inside the paper, not from generic disclaimers. Name the specific condition or dataset where the result stops holding.

## Sentence Syntax

Discussion sentences interpret:

- `may reflect`
- `suggests that`
- `could indicate`
- `is likely due to`
- `may facilitate`

Do not drift into Results syntax (`was detected`, `increased`, `achieved`) unless intentionally restating a finding before interpreting it.

## Widening Sequence

1. Central advance — restate the key finding in interpretive terms.
2. Why the evidence supports it — connect mechanism to observation.
3. How it changes a workflow, design rule, or conceptual boundary.
4. How it relates to previous studies — agreement, disagreement, extension.
5. What limits or dependencies remain — specific, not generic.
6. What future work is now plausible — concrete, not marketing.

## Short Rule to Memorize

- **Results** = what we observed
- **Discussion** = how we understand it, and when it may fail

## Common Failure Modes

- Re-summarizing Results instead of interpreting them.
- Skipping rival explanations.
- Omitting boundaries — when does the interpretation stop holding?
- Future-work statements that read as marketing, not as honest open questions.
- Using mechanism verbs (`demonstrates`, `proves`, `establishes`) backed by correlational evidence.

## Diagnostics Before Finalizing

- Does each interpretive claim trace back to specific evidence in this paper?
- Are mechanism verbs calibrated to study design strength?
- Is the scope of the implication narrower than or equal to the scope of the evidence?
- Is any "first" claim genuinely first within a stated scope?
